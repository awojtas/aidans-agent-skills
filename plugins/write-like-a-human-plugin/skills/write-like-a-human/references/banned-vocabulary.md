# Banned AI Vocabulary

These words and phrases are so overused by AI that they function as a fingerprint. Do not use them unless the context absolutely demands the specific word and no alternative exists.

## Banned words

delve, crucial, pivotal, vital, vibrant, tapestry, landscape (metaphorical), testament, underscore (as a verb), intricate/intricacies, interplay, meticulous/meticulously, showcase, foster, garner, bolster, enhance, enduring, embark, leverage, comprehensive, groundbreaking, encompasses, multifaceted, notable, noteworthy, renowned, innovative, realm, nuanced, align (with), boast (meaning "has"), highlight (as a verb), nestled, resonate (with), focal point, indelible, deeply rooted, valuable (as in "valuable insights"), featuring, cultivate

## Overused creative writing words

AI creative writing gravitates to the same set of "atmospheric" words. Avoid overusing:

quiet, chaos, violence, brittle, raw, searing, ache, rhythmic, insistent, steady, delicate, whisper (as a metaphor), gleaming, shattered, fractured, hollow

These aren't banned outright the way the list above is - they're real words that real writers use. But AI uses them constantly and in clusters. If you've used two of them in the same paragraph, that's a sign you're drifting into AI voice.

## Overused AI imagery patterns

- Weather similes (storms, rain, lightning, thunder) for emotional states
- Light/dark metaphors for good/evil or hope/despair
- Scent descriptions using the "something X, something Y" construction
- Fire/burning metaphors for passion or anger

These aren't off-limits, but reach for something more specific and less stock. The test: could this simile apply to basically any situation? If yes, it's too generic.

## Banned phrases

- "It's important to note that"
- "It's worth mentioning"
- "serves as a" / "stands as a"
- "a testament to"
- "reflects broader"
- "plays a crucial/pivotal role"
- "in the heart of"
- "a diverse array of"
- "the evolving landscape of"
- "setting the stage for"
- "marking a shift"
- "shaping the future of"
- "nestled in"
- "commitment to"
- "rich history"
- "natural beauty"
- "it is not just X - it is Y"
- "not merely X but Y"
- "despite these challenges"
- "looking ahead"
- "symbolizing its ongoing/enduring"
- "contributing to the" (when tacked onto the end of a sentence as a participle tail)
- "focal point"
- "indelible mark"
- "deeply rooted"
- "valuable insights"
- "resonates/aligns with"
- "maintains an active social media presence"
- "the short version is" / "the short version:"

**Chatbot voice phrases** (especially relevant for emails and conversational text):

- "I hope this helps"
- "Certainly!" / "Of course!"
- "You're absolutely right!"
- "Would you like me to..."
- "Let me know if"
- "Is there anything else"
- "Here is a"

**Knowledge-gap hedging** (don't dress up uncertainty in formal language):

- "While specific details are limited/scarce"
- "based on available information"
- "not widely documented/disclosed"
- "keeps much of their personal life private"

## Banned sentence-starting transitions

Do not start sentences with these mechanical AI connectors:

- "Additionally,"
- "Furthermore,"
- "Moreover,"
- "It is worth noting that"
- "Importantly,"

### What to use instead

- "Also" / "And" / "Plus" / "On top of that"
- Or just start the next thought without a transition at all
