# Guardrails (read before any outward-facing action)

Automated marketing slides into spam, bans, and legal trouble faster than almost anything else an agent does. These rules are non-negotiable across every skill in this bundle.

## The human-in-the-loop boundary

- **Agents PREPARE. Humans POST anything public.** Draft the Reddit comment, the LinkedIn post, the Product Hunt copy, the cold email — then hand it to the human to send. Do not auto-post to public platforms.
- **Automate freely the safe stuff:** research, analysis, metrics pulls, draft generation, doc updates, asset specs.
- This boundary can loosen per-app over time once the human trusts the output, but it is the default and it is opt-out, not opt-in.

## Anti-spam / platform rules

- **Reddit and forums:** value-first only. Answer a real question where the product is genuinely the best answer. Most subreddits ban or shadowban overt self-promotion. Read each sub's self-promo rule before posting. One helpful comment beats ten links.
- **Product Hunt / directories:** one honest listing each. No fake upvotes, no vote rings — it gets the launch killed.
- **No engagement faking** anywhere (bought upvotes, sockpuppets, fake reviews). It is fraud and it is brittle.

## Legal / privacy

- **Email:** comply with anti-spam law (CAN-SPAM, CASL, etc.) — only mail people who opted in, always include a real unsubscribe and sender address.
- **Personal data:** the moment you store someone's personal data (an email list, scraped contacts) you are under privacy law (GDPR, NZ Privacy Act, and equivalents). Collect only what you need, for a stated purpose, with consent.
- **Scraping for cold outreach is off-limits.** Scraping public discussion to *listen* (themes, language, sentiment) is low-risk. Scraping people's profiles to build a cold-contact list and email them is against platform terms and drags in privacy law. Listening yes; cold lists no.

## Honesty

- Market the product that exists, not the one you wish existed. No invented features, fake testimonials, or inflated metrics. The fastest way to lose a small audience is to oversell.
