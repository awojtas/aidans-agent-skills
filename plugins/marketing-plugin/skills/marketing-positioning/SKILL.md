# Positioning and messaging

The most leverage per word, and the easiest step to skip. Everything downstream — landing copy, posts, SEO, ads — inherits from this. Get it wrong and you pay for it in every asset.

## When to use

After research, before assets. Re-run when the product or audience shifts.

## Process

1. **Read `profile.md` and `research-findings.md`.**
2. **Find the wedge.** The one specific job this app does better than the obvious alternative. Not "another X tool" — the sharp thing. Pull it straight from how users described their pain.
3. **Name the enemy.** The status quo you displace (a competitor, a spreadsheet, a manual process). Naming it makes the pitch concrete.
4. **State the proof.** Why believe it — the demo moment, the before/after, early results.
5. **Write the do-not-say list.** The over-claims and jargon to avoid (anti-positioning is as useful as positioning).
6. **Write `docs/marketing/positioning.md`** — wedge sentence, enemy, proof, do-not-say. Update `profile.md`'s `one_liner`.

## Output

`positioning.md` and an updated `one_liner`. This is the brief `marketing-assets` writes against.

Lean on the verbatim language from research — borrowed words convert better than invented ones.
